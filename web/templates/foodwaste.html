Food Waste

  • Composting: This is a natural process of decomposing organic matter, including food waste, into a nutrient-rich soil amendment. You can compost food waste in your backyard using a compost bin or pile, or you can use a commercial composting service.

  • Anaerobic Digestion: This is a process of breaking down organic matter, including food waste, in the absence of oxygen. This process produces biogas, which can be used as a renewable energy source. Anaerobic digestion can be done on a small scale using a home biogas digester or on a larger scale using a commercial digester.

  • Donation: If you have excess food that is still edible, consider donating it to a local food bank or charity.

  • Animal Feed: Food waste can be used as animal feed for pigs, chickens, and other livestock. Check with local farmers or animal rescues to see if they accept food waste.
